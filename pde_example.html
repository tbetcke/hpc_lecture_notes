The need for sparse linear algebra - A PDE example¶

In modern applications we are dealing with matrices that have hundreds of thousands to billions of unknowns. A typical feature of these matrices is that they are highly sparse. Typically, by sparsity we mean that a matrix mostly consists of zero elements so that it is more economical not to store the whole matrix, but just the nonzero entries. This makes necessary different types of storage structures and algorithms to deal with these matrices and to efficiently exploit the sparsity property.

Before we dive into sparse matrix storage format, we want to give a simple example that demonstrates the necessity of sparse matrix formats and algorithms to deal with them.
